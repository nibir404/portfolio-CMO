# Abdullah Al Alamin — New Landing Page: Content & SEO Blueprint

## What I found on the current site
The live site (abdullah-al-alamin.vercel.app) is a multi-page personal-brand site: Home, About, Experience, Professional Photos, CV, Talk Shows, News & Press, Articles, Contact. The positioning is consistent throughout: **"Legacy is Greater than Currency"** as a personal tagline, with a career arc from FMCG event execution (PRAN-RFL, 2012) → brand & eMedia leadership (Daffodil Group) → Brand Developer & Media Lead (Metrocem) → Head of Brand & Communication (Bengal Group) → current role as **Head of Marketing, Brand & Corporate Affairs at Betopia Group** (functioning as Group CMO). The strongest proof points are concrete numbers (350% sales growth, 1,500+ youth participants, 3 international awards, 12 business units managed) and media credibility (a Channel i talk show, national press features, government/ambassador-level speaking).

The gap: this content is scattered across 8 pages, so no single page currently ranks well for "who is Abdullah Al Alamin" or "brand strategist Bangladesh," and there's no clear call-to-action for the audience that matters most (companies/brands wanting to hire or partner with him).

---

## Recommended section count: **9 sections**, single scrolling page

A one-page format works well here because his story is linear and the proof points are punchy — but 9 sections is the right number to avoid two failure modes: too few (feels like a thin bio) or too many (dilutes SEO focus and hurts scroll completion). Each section below maps to one clear search/user intent.

1. Hero
2. About / Positioning statement
3. Career Timeline (the journey)
4. Key Accomplishments (proof/numbers)
5. Areas of Expertise (services-style block — this is what turns a bio into a landing page)
6. Media, Press & Speaking
7. Education & Credentials
8. Testimonials / Endorsements *(placeholder — recommend collecting 2-3 quotes)*
9. Contact / CTA + Newsletter

Optional 10th: an "Articles/Insights" teaser strip if he wants ongoing SEO content — but I'd treat that as a stretch goal, not core.

---

## SEO strategy for the page

**Primary target keyword:** `Abdullah Al Alamin` (branded search — already has intent, needs authority)
**Secondary keywords to weave in naturally:** `brand strategist Bangladesh`, `Head of Marketing Betopia Group`, `Group CMO Bangladesh`, `FMCG brand communication expert`, `corporate affairs leader Bangladesh`

**Recommended `<title>` tag (under 60 characters):**
`Abdullah Al Alamin | Brand Strategist & Group CMO, Betopia`

**Recommended meta description (under 155 characters):**
`Abdullah Al Alamin is a Bangladesh-based brand strategist and Head of Marketing, Brand & Corporate Affairs at Betopia Group. 13+ years, 350% growth campaigns.`

**H1 (one per page, exact-match branded + descriptor):**
`Abdullah Al Alamin — Brand Architect, Group CMO & Digital Transformation Leader`

**Structural SEO notes:**
- Use exactly one `<h1>` (hero), then `<h2>` for each of the 9 section titles, `<h3>` for sub-items (timeline years, expertise areas, education entries).
- Add `Person` schema (JSON-LD) with `jobTitle`, `worksFor` (Betopia Group), `alumniOf` (DIIT), `sameAs` (LinkedIn, press links) — this is what gets a knowledge-panel-style rich result for branded search.
- Keep all images with descriptive `alt` text, e.g. `alt="Abdullah Al Alamin speaking at Betopia Group brand strategy event"` — not `alt="Hero.webp"`.
- Internal-link out from this single page to deeper assets he already has (CV download, full press page, articles) — good for dwell time and existing page equity, even though the landing page itself is one-pager.
- Target page load: compress hero image (currently a large `Hero.webp` at full 3840px width — serve responsive sizes).
- Since this is a single URL, all SEO weight concentrates on one page — good for branded search, but he'll want the **Articles** section to eventually branch into its own indexed content for competitive terms like "brand strategist Bangladesh," since a bio page alone won't outrank content-rich competitors on non-branded terms.
